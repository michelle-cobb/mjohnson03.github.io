Northern Cornbread

Pennsylvania Dutch | Recipes | Sides

Michelle

Michelle’s Northern Cornbread

Also known as Yankee Cornbread, some folks get fired up about differences between cornbreads in the North and South! I don’t fuss about which one is “real” cornbread, because they’re two different breads. In the North most people use yellow instead of white cornmeal, and this recipe yields a softer crust because it uses a pan instead of a preheated cast iron skillet. The flour and sugar, which are no-no’s for many Southern cornbread cooks, result in a slightly sweet, well-binded bread.

This recipe serves 6-8.  Baking time is about 30 minutes.

Original Source

I adapted the Johnny Cake recipe from a free EBook offered by The Project Gutenberg, called Pennsylvania Dutch Cooking. It’s a nice collection of old Pennsylvania Dutch recipes. The name Johnny Cake isn’t really correct though, because Johnny Cakes are unleavened and pan-fried instead of baked.

How I Made it Vegan

I replaced the 2 eggs with 1/2 cup vegan sour cream, which is a nice egg substitute in quick bread recipes. Eggs have leavening properties, so I also added a pinch more baking soda to help it rise.

I replaced the sour milk/buttermilk with a vinegar/soy milk mixture. This is a common way to make vegan sour milk.

Other Changes

I changed the instructions to say that the batter should only be mixed until just moistened. I also added tests to see when it’s done baking, and I shortened the estimated baking time.

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp white vinegar
  • less than 1 and 1/4 cups plain soy milk
  • 1 and 1/2 cups yellow cornmeal
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 and 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 3/4 tsp and 1 pinch baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 Tbsp vegan sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegan sour cream
  • 1/4 cup vegetable shortening, melted. Crisco is vegan, in case you were wondering.

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375F.
  2. Combine the vinegar with enough soy milk to yield 1 and 1/4 cup, and set aside. Make sure it sits at least 5 minutes before you use it in step #5.
  3. Grease an 8x8x2 inch pan with shortening.
  4. Sift the cornmeal, flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and sugar together into a large mixing bowl.
  5. Combine vegan sour cream and the soy milk/vinegar mixture in a small mixing bowl, then add to the cornmeal mixture.
  6. Mix with a spoon just until the dry ingredients are moistened (batter will be lumpy).
  7. Stir in the melted shortening.
  8. Pour into pan and bake. You’ll know it’s done when the edges turn golden brown and begin to pull away from the sides of the pan, and a toothpick inserted in the middle comes out clean (about 30 minutes).
  9. Serve warm, with vegan butter. Will keep well for a couple of days stored at room temperature.
